About

I'm a PhD candidate at Texas Tech University working at the frontier of Federated Learning and Large Language Models. My core question: how do we build AI systems that are both powerful and trustworthy. Especially when the data, the compute, and the people are all distributed?

My current work focuses on adversarial robustness in FL-LLM pipelines. To be specific model poisoning attacks, explanation-guided defences, and privacy-preserving aggregation strategies. I'm also exploring machine unlearning as a path toward verifiable, regulation-compliant AI.

Before the PhD I spent time with sensor data and deep learning for human activity recognition which gave me a healthy appreciation for the messy gap between benchmark performance and real-world deployment.
